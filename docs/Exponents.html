Exponents

Exponential Form

Exponential form is expressed as \(a^n\), where \(a\) is the base and \(n\) is the exponent.

Example: \(2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8\)

Worked Example

Calculate \(4^2\): Show Answer Answer: \(4^2 = 4 \times 4 = 16\)

Expanded Form

Expanded form shows the multiplication explicitly.

Example: \(3^4 = 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 81\)

Worked Example

Expand \(5^3\): Show Answer Answer: \(5^3 = 5 \times 5 \times 5 = 125\)

Standard Form

Standard form is the numerical value of an exponent.

Example: \(5^2 = 25\)

Worked Example

Convert \(2^5\): Show Answer Answer: \(2^5 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 32\)

Practice Exercises

1. Write \(6^2\) in exponential form and calculate it. Show Answer Answer: \(6^2 = 36\)

2. Expand \(2^4\) and calculate it. Show Answer Answer: \(2^4 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 16\)

3. What is the standard form of \(3^3\)? Show Answer Answer: \(3^3 = 27\)
